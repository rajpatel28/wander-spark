Montreal

Top things to do and experience

Old Montreal (Vieux-Montréal)

Old Montreal

Highlights: Cobblestone streets, historic architecture, cafés, river views.

Cost: Free

Tips: Go early morning for the best photos and empty streets.

Notre-Dame Basilica

Highlights: Stunning blue-and-gold interior, Gothic Revival design, the Aura light show.

Cost: ~$15 (Aura show extra)

Tips: Buy tickets online — lines can get crazy.

Old Port of Montreal

Highlights: Waterfront promenade, street performers, ferris wheel, lively atmosphere.

Cost: Free (activities extra)

Tips: Visit around sunset for great views and photos.

La Grande Roue

Highlights: 60-meter ferris wheel with panoramic views of the city.

Cost: ~$20–25

Tips: Go on a clear day — reflections ruin night photos.

Downtown & Mount Royal

Underground City (RESO)

Highlights: 33 km of tunnels connecting malls, metros, eateries, and plazas.

Cost: Free

Tips: Perfect for rainy or freezing days — it’s huge, so follow signage.

St. Catherine Street

Highlights: Montreal’s main shopping and nightlife strip.

Cost: Free to explore

Tips: Pedestrian-only sections in summer are great for cafés and people-watching.

Montreal Museum of Fine Arts

Highlights: Canada’s largest art museum, sculptures, world-class temporary exhibits.

Cost: ~$25

Tips: Free admission for the sculpture garden outside.

Mount Royal Lookout

Highlights: Best skyline view in Montreal, overlooking downtown and the river.

Cost: Free

Tips: Sunset is unreal — but sunrise has zero crowds.

Mount Royal Park

Highlights: Scenic trails, wildlife, lake, picnic spots.

Cost: Free

Tips: The loop around Beaver Lake is peaceful and less touristy.

St. Joseph’s Oratory

Highlights: Canada’s largest church, domed basilica, panoramic terrace.

Cost: Free entry (donation encouraged)

Tips: The viewpoint behind the basilica is a hidden gem.

Plateau & Mile-End

Square Saint-Louis

Highlights: Famous pastel houses, romantic park, leafy paths.

Cost: Free

Tips: Great for photos in the afternoon when the light hits the façades.

Mile-End

Highlights: Vintage shops, indie cafés, murals, artsy vibes.

Cost: Free to explore

Tips: Try St-Viateur or Fairmount for authentic Montreal bagels.

St-Viateur Bagel

Highlights: Iconic Montreal-style wood-fired bagels.

Cost: Price varies

Tips: They bake 24/7 — late-night bagels hit different.

Montreal Street Art (St-Laurent)

Highlights: Massive murals, art festivals, colorful blocks.

Cost: Free

Tips: The MURAL Festival in June adds new pieces yearly.

Little Italy & Markets

Jean-Talon Market

Highlights: Local produce, spices, bakeries, flowers.

Cost: Free entry

Tips: Vendors often offer samples — taste everything.

Little Italy

Highlights: Espresso bars, trattorias, Italian grocery stores.

Cost: Free

Tips: Grab gelato from a local shop nearby.

Olympic Park & Surroundings

Biodôme

Highlights: Walk through recreated ecosystems from rainforest to arctic.

Cost: ~$22–30

Tips: Weekdays are WAY less crowded.

Planetarium

Highlights: Immersive shows, astronomy exhibits.

Cost: ~$20–25

Tips: Arrive 15 min early — shows do not allow late entry.

Montreal Tower Observatory

Highlights: World’s tallest inclined tower with panoramic windows.

Cost: ~$22

Tips: Visit on a clear day — fog ruins the view.
